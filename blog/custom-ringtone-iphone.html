How to Set a Custom Ringtone on iPhone (2026 Guide)

July 10, 2026 7 min read SeZZamE Team

iPhones come with dozens of ringtones, none of which sound like anything you'd deliberately choose. Setting a custom ringtone — your own audio, a song clip, a sound you recorded — should be straightforward. Apple has made it surprisingly complicated.

This guide covers every method that actually works in 2026: using GarageBand on iPhone, using Finder on Mac, and a quick alternative for alarm sounds specifically. No paid apps required.

What you need to know first

iPhone ringtones use the .m4r file format — an AAC audio file with a renamed extension. The file must be 40 seconds or shorter (this is an Apple-enforced limit for ringtones). Longer files will be rejected.

The file has to be transferred to your iPhone via iTunes or Finder, or created directly on-device using GarageBand. You cannot simply AirDrop an audio file and set it as a ringtone — iOS doesn't allow it through the standard share sheet.

Method 1: GarageBand on iPhone (no Mac needed)

This is the most self-contained method — everything happens on your iPhone. You need GarageBand installed (it's free from Apple).

  1. Open GarageBand on your iPhone.
  2. Create a new project. Choose any instrument — Audio Recorder works well.
  3. Tap the loop browser icon (top right) and switch to the Files tab.
  4. Navigate to the audio file you want to use as a ringtone. If it's not there yet, add it to your Files app first (iCloud Drive, Downloads, etc.).
  5. Drag the audio file into the GarageBand track view.
  6. Trim the clip to 40 seconds or less by dragging the right edge of the region.
  7. Tap the down arrow at the top of the screen, then tap My Songs to save the project.
  8. In the My Songs view, long-press the project you just saved, then tap Share.
  9. Tap Ringtone, give it a name, and tap Export.
  10. When the export finishes, tap Use sound as… and choose Standard Ringtone, Standard Text Tone, or assign it to a specific contact.

Your custom ringtone is now in Settings → Sounds & Haptics → Ringtone under the Custom section.

Method 2: Finder on Mac (most reliable)

If you have a Mac, this method gives you more control over audio editing. You'll prepare the file on your Mac and sync it to your iPhone.

Step 1: Prepare the audio file

  1. Open the audio file in QuickTime Player or any audio editor (GarageBand on Mac, Audacity, etc.).
  2. Trim it to the section you want — maximum 40 seconds.
  3. Export it as an AAC (.m4a) file. In QuickTime: File → Export As → Audio Only. In GarageBand on Mac: Share → Export Song to Disk → AAC File.

Step 2: Rename the file extension

  1. In Finder, right-click the exported .m4a file and choose Get Info, or just click the filename to rename it.
  2. Change the file extension from .m4a to .m4r.
  3. If macOS warns you about changing the extension, click Use .m4r.

Step 3: Sync to iPhone via Finder

  1. Connect your iPhone to your Mac with a USB cable.
  2. Open Finder and click your iPhone in the left sidebar under Locations.
  3. Click the General tab, then scroll down to the Tones section. If you don't see a Tones tab, drag the .m4r file directly onto your iPhone in the Finder sidebar.
  4. Drag your .m4r file into the Tones section (or onto your device icon in the sidebar).
  5. Click Sync or Apply.

After syncing, open Settings → Sounds & Haptics → Ringtone on your iPhone. Your custom ringtone will appear under the Custom section at the top of the list.

Windows users: The process is the same using iTunes for Windows instead of Finder. Connect your iPhone, open iTunes, click the device icon, and look for the Tones section. Drag your .m4r file into the Tones list and click Sync.

Setting the ringtone

Once your custom tone is on your iPhone:

  • Default ringtone: Settings → Sounds & Haptics → Ringtone → select your tone under Custom.
  • Per-contact ringtone: Open Contacts → select the contact → Edit → Ringtone → choose your custom tone.
  • Text tone: Settings → Sounds & Haptics → Text Tone → Custom section.

Common issues and fixes

The Tones section doesn't appear in Finder

This happens when your iPhone is set up for wireless syncing. Try dragging the .m4r file directly onto the iPhone icon in the Finder sidebar. Alternatively, enable manual sync: in Finder, click your iPhone → General tab → check "Manually manage music and videos."

Ringtone not showing up on iPhone after sync

Make sure the file extension is genuinely .m4r (not .m4a renamed with hidden extensions still active). In Finder, turn on View → Show All Filename Extensions to confirm. Also verify the file is shorter than 40 seconds — files longer than that silently fail.

File won't export from GarageBand as Ringtone

The Ringtone export option only appears if the project's audio track is 40 seconds or shorter. Trim the track in GarageBand until the option becomes available.

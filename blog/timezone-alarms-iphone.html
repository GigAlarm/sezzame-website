How to Schedule Alarms Across Time Zones on iPhone

July 3, 2026 6 min read SeZZamE Team

Here's a scenario that happens every day: you have a standing 10 AM New York call. You're in San Francisco. You set an alarm for 7 AM — the correct offset today. Next week you're in London. The alarm fires at 7 AM London time, four hours after your meeting already started.

The iPhone's built-in Clock app doesn't understand timezones. Every alarm it creates is anchored to your device's current local time, which means the moment you cross a timezone boundary, previously correct alarms become wrong.

SeZZamE solves this with timezone-aware alarms. Here's how they work and why they matter.

Why the built-in Clock app fails across timezones

When you set an alarm in the iOS Clock app for "7:00 AM," it stores that as 7:00 AM in whatever timezone your device is currently set to. If your device timezone changes — because you traveled, because iOS adjusted automatically, or because you manually switched it — the alarm still fires at 7:00 AM in the new timezone. There's no way to say "this alarm should always fire at 10:00 AM Eastern regardless of where I am."

This isn't a bug; it's a design limitation. The Clock app was built for a single device in a single timezone. Most people don't travel enough to notice. But if you do — or if your work involves regular communication with people in other time zones — it creates constant friction.

How SeZZamE timezone alarms work

Every alarm in SeZZamE can be pinned to a specific timezone. When you create or edit an alarm, you'll see a timezone selector. Choose "America/New_York" (or any of hundreds of supported zones) and SeZZamE stores the alarm as "10:00 AM Eastern."

No matter where you are when the alarm fires — San Francisco, London, Tokyo — SeZZamE calculates the correct local moment for that alarm to go off. If the New York 10 AM call happens at 7 AM Pacific, the alarm fires at 7 AM Pacific when you're on the West Coast. If you're in London for a month, it fires at 3 PM London time. The alarm always fires at the right moment.

Key point: SeZZamE timezone alarms are zone-anchored, not offset-anchored. This means they automatically handle Daylight Saving Time transitions correctly. A "10 AM Eastern" alarm fires at 10 AM Eastern regardless of whether DST is in effect.

Setting a timezone alarm in SeZZamE: step by step

  1. Open SeZZamE and tap the + button to create a new alarm, or tap an existing alarm to edit it.
  2. In the alarm detail screen, set your time — for example, 10:00 AM.
  3. Scroll to the Timezone section and tap it.
  4. Search for your target timezone by city or timezone name — "New York," "London," "Tokyo," "UTC," etc.
  5. Select the timezone and save the alarm.

SeZZamE will show you what time the alarm corresponds to in your current local time so you can confirm it looks right before saving.

Common use cases for timezone alarms

Remote work and recurring cross-timezone meetings

If you have a standing 9 AM Pacific standup with your West Coast team and you're based in New York, set the alarm in SeZZamE pinned to America/Los_Angeles. The alarm fires at 12 PM Eastern on regular days — and on days you're traveling through Europe, it fires at the correct local moment for that timezone too.

International travel

Before a trip to London, set your important alarms — morning routine, meeting times, flight departure — pinned to Europe/London. When you land and your phone adjusts to BST, those alarms fire at exactly the right times without you having to touch anything.

Family and friends in other timezones

Scheduling a weekly video call with family in Australia? Pin that alarm to Australia/Sydney and it will always fire at the right moment for you to join, accounting for Australia's Daylight Saving quirks (which run opposite to the Northern Hemisphere) automatically.

Event-specific alerts

Bought tickets to a live-streamed concert happening at 8 PM GMT? Create a SeZZamE alarm pinned to Europe/London at 8 PM. It fires at 3 PM Eastern, 8 PM London time — no mental math required.

Combining timezone alarms with Plans

SeZZamE's Plans feature lets you build multi-day schedules. Each alarm within a Plan can have its own timezone. So you can build a full conference schedule for an event in Tokyo, with every session pinned to Asia/Tokyo time, and the alarms will fire correctly regardless of where you physically are during the conference.

If the conference schedule changes, drag the entire Plan to the new start date and every alarm — and their timezone anchors — shift together.

Never Miss Another Meeting: Plan Better and Always Arrive on Time

July 10, 2026 6 min read SeZZamE Team

Being late to a meeting isn't usually a time-management failure. It's a planning failure. You knew the meeting was at 2 PM. The problem was the 20-minute drive, the 10 minutes of parking, and the fact that your previous call ran over — none of which you built into your schedule.

The fix isn't to try harder or be more disciplined. The fix is to build a smarter alarm system around your day so that the right reminders fire at the right moments — before you leave, not when you're already late.

Why people miss meetings (even with calendar apps)

Calendar apps like Google Calendar and Apple Calendar show you when something starts. They don't help you work backwards from that time to figure out when you need to stop what you're doing, when to leave, or when to wrap up the previous meeting.

A 2 PM meeting that requires a 25-minute drive means you need to leave by 1:30 PM. If you have a 1 PM call, that call needs to end by 1:25 PM. That chain of dependencies lives entirely in your head — and when you're deep in work, your head is not a reliable alarm clock.

What you actually need are multiple timed alerts that map to your real workflow, not just the start time on the invite.

The four-alarm system for never being late

For any meeting that requires travel or preparation, set four alarms instead of one:

  1. Wrap-up alarm — fires when you need to finish what you're currently doing. For a 30-minute pre-meeting prep window, this might be 90 minutes before the meeting.
  2. Prep alarm — fires when it's time to review your notes, pull up the agenda, or get your materials together. Usually 30–45 minutes out.
  3. Leave alarm — fires when you need to physically walk out the door. Travel time + buffer, subtracted from the meeting time.
  4. Arrival alarm — fires 5 minutes before the meeting starts, when you should already be at the location and composed. Your last check-in before walking in.

This sounds like a lot of setup for one meeting. The key is to build this once as a reusable Plan in SeZZamE, then copy and adjust it for each meeting.

Building a meeting day Plan in SeZZamE

SeZZamE's Plans feature lets you create a named multi-alarm schedule and reuse it across days. Here's how to set one up for a typical workday with two external meetings:

  1. Open the Plans tab in SeZZamE.
  2. Tap + to create a new Plan. Name it something like "Tuesday Meetings" or "Client Day."
  3. For Day 1, add your alarm chain for the first meeting: wrap-up, prep, leave, arrive.
  4. Add the same four-alarm chain for your second meeting in the same day.

The next time you have a similar day, duplicate the Plan and adjust the times. You don't rebuild from scratch.

Location attachments: directions one tap away

SeZZamE lets you attach a location to any alarm. When the alarm fires, you see the location name directly on the alert. Tap it and Maps (or your preferred navigation app) opens with directions already loaded from your current position.

For a meeting at a new office, a client's building, or a restaurant you've never been to, this eliminates the fumble of searching for the address while you're already running behind. The alarm fires, you tap, you navigate.

You can also attach a link — useful for virtual meetings. Set the leave alarm with the Zoom or Teams link attached, so when it fires, one tap opens the call.

Planning for back-to-back meetings

Back-to-back meetings are where punctuality systems usually break down. The first meeting runs 8 minutes over, which cascades into every subsequent one.

The fix is an explicit wrap-up alarm for each meeting, not just a start alarm for the next one. Set a "5 minutes to go" alarm that fires near the end of the first meeting — a cue to start wrapping up the conversation, not a panic signal that the next thing has already started.

In SeZZamE, give this alarm a distinct sound so you recognise it immediately without looking at your phone. A gentle tone for the wrap-up, a sharper one for the leave alarm.

The night-before habit

The most effective version of this system takes two minutes the evening before. Open SeZZamE, check your Plans for tomorrow, and adjust any alarm times that don't match what's on your calendar. Move a leave alarm 10 minutes earlier if traffic will be heavy. Silence an alarm if a meeting got cancelled.

This nightly review replaces the morning scramble of realising you have three meetings and no clear plan for how to reach any of them on time.

One thing to change today: For your next external meeting, set a dedicated "leave by" alarm — not just a meeting start reminder. Build in the actual travel time plus a 10-minute buffer. That single change will make you noticeably more punctual within a week.
